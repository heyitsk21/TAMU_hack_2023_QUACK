Moon Phases

Hey there, curious minds! There are many different categories of science. Let' s start with one of the coolest things in the night sky ‚Äì the Moon and its different phases.

The Moon doesn't shine on its own; it reflects the light of the Sun. As the Moon orbits Earth, different portions of it are illuminated by the Sun, creating what we call "moon phases."

There are eight main phases of the Moon: New Moon, Waxing Crescent, First Quarter, Waxing Gibbous, Full Moon, Waning Gibbous, Last Quarter, and Waning Crescent. Each phase has a unique appearance, and it takes about 29.5 days for the Moon to complete its entire cycle.

During a New Moon, the side facing Earth is not visible because it is not illuminated. As the days go by, we see more and more of the illuminated part, leading up to the Full Moon, where the entire side facing Earth is lit up.

So, the next time you look up at the night sky and see the Moon, observe its shape and see if you can identify which phase it's in. It's like the Moon is playing a cosmic game of hide-and-seek with the Sun!
